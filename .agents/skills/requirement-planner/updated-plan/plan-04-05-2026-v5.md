## 1. My understanding of the illegal-style activities

> **Note:** This is **my understanding** of the illegal-style activities the system must catch. The PRD lists the four names only; the explanations below are derived from standard MT5 broker practice and the conversation in [conversation-with-chatgpt.md](.agents/skills/requirement-planner/conversation-with-chatgpt.md). They are working definitions, not final rulings — Alex and the compliance/team lead must review and confirm them.

The PRD requires the MVP to support **exactly four** detection types ([PRD §3](.agents/skills/requirement-planner/requirement-docs/BestWingGlobal_MT5_AI_Risk_Control_Agent_MVP_PRD_EN.md)). Below is what each one means in plain English, why it harms the broker, and the patterns a detector (rule-based or LLM) should look for.

### 1.1 Latency Arbitrage

**What it is.** A trader uses a faster external price feed than the one the broker uses. They see a price move on the fast feed first, open a position with us **before** our price catches up, then close the position seconds later for a near risk-free profit. It is essentially "I already know where the price is going" — not because of skill, but because of a feed timing gap.

**Why it harms the broker.** Each winning trade is paid out of the broker's pocket. There is no real risk taken by the trader, so the broker carries the loss every time.

**Patterns to look for.**

- Many sub-minute trades with a very high win rate (well above the ~50% baseline).
- Entries clustered milliseconds before our quoted price moves.
- Profit consistently in the trader's favor across the request-to-execution gap (slippage favors them, never us).
- Concentration on highly liquid instruments where external feeds tend to lead (EURUSD, XAUUSD, etc.).
- Trades clustered around scheduled news releases (sub-pattern: news arbitrage).

### 1.2 Scalping Violation

**What it is.** The broker has a written rule (often per MT5 group) that a trade must be held for at least a minimum amount of time, e.g. 60 seconds. Scalping violation means the trader deliberately closes trades faster than that minimum, harvesting many tiny edges that the rule was designed to prevent.

**Why it harms the broker.** Scalping below the minimum hold time exploits pricing tolerances and execution behavior the broker offers in good faith for normal trading. It also breaches the trader's account agreement.

**Patterns to look for.**

- High percentage of trades closed below the group's minimum hold time.
- Repeated, mechanical timing (open, hold a few seconds, close — over and over).
- A meaningful share of total profit comes from those rule-violating trades.
- Repeat offender: prior warnings on the CRM record.

### 1.3 Swap Arbitrage

**What it is.** Brokers pay or charge **swap** (overnight rollover interest) on positions held across a daily cutoff. A swap-arbitrage trader opens offsetting positions — for example long one symbol and short a correlated one, sometimes split across two or more accounts owned by the same person — so the **net market exposure is close to zero** but the trader still **collects positive swap**. They essentially earn interest with no market risk.

**Why it harms the broker.** The broker is paying real interest for what looks like a hedged book on its side, but the trader has constructed it specifically to extract that interest with no real economic risk-taking.

**Patterns to look for.**

- Net delta exposure ≈ 0 across correlated pairs, while gross exposure is large.
- Most of the account's profit comes from **swap income**, not from price movement.
- Positions consistently opened **just before** and closed **just after** the swap-credit cutoff.
- Multiple linked accounts (same KYC name, IP, or device fingerprint) holding mirrored hedges.

### 1.4 Bonus / Credit Abuse

**What it is.** The broker grants promotional money or credit (deposit-match bonus, no-deposit bonus, loyalty credit) with rules attached — for example, "you must trade $50,000 in volume before withdrawing". Bonus abuse is when the trader breaks the spirit of those rules. The three common variants:

- **Volume churn:** opening and closing offsetting trades that produce near-zero P&L just to inflate the volume counter and unlock the bonus.
- **Quick withdraw:** depositing a small amount, claiming a bonus, and immediately requesting a withdrawal before the turnover requirement is genuinely met.
- **Multi-account ring:** opening many sibling/duplicate accounts (often by the same person under different names, or by a small group sharing IPs/devices) to claim the same bonus repeatedly.

**Why it harms the broker.** The bonus is a marketing cost that the broker pays expecting genuine trading activity in return. Abuse turns the bonus into a direct cash giveaway.

**Patterns to look for.**

- Large volume of near-zero-P&L round trips appearing only after the bonus was granted.
- Pending withdrawal request shortly after a bonus grant or a small deposit.
- Bonus amount disproportionate to the deposit (e.g. $10 deposit → $500 bonus claim).
- Multiple accounts sharing IP / device fingerprint / KYC name.
- Withdrawal-attempt frequency spikes after the bonus.

## 2. Related activities mentioned in the PRD but not standalone cases

PRD §2 (Event Trigger Scan) also names three behaviors that are **warning signs**, not separate detection types. They feed into the four cases above:

- **Abnormal short-term profit** — sudden, statistically unusual profit spike. Strong input to **Latency Arbitrage** and **Scalping Violation**.
- **High-frequency trading detected** — burst of orders per second above a threshold. Input to **Latency Arbitrage** and **Scalping Violation**.
- **News-event trading window triggered** — trading clustered around scheduled high-impact news. Sub-pattern of **Latency Arbitrage**.

These three are triggers for instant scans; they are not new cases the officer reviews on their own.

## 3. How this section is used downstream

- **Rule engine (v3 §2):** every parameter must trace back to one or more of these four definitions. If a parameter does not help detect any of them, it should not be in the config.
- **LLM prompts (v4 §2):** the verdict line must use the vocabulary from §1 (e.g. _"Pattern is consistent with latency arbitrage"_).
- **Case schema:** the `risk_type` field is restricted to `{ latency_arbitrage, scalping_violation, swap_arbitrage, bonus_abuse }`. Anything outside this set requires a new plan version and a PRD update.
- **Telegram alert:** the **Risk Type** field shown to the officer is one of these four labels exactly.
